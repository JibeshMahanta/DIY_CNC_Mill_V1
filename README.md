# DIY CNC Mill V1

## Description
A CNC Mill made using Arduino UNO, GRBL, NEMA17 stepper motors, 3D printed parts
Inspired by designs by NikodemBartnik https://indystry.cc/author/nikodembartnik/

## Hardware
- Arduino UNO
- NEMA17 stepper motors
- DRV8825 stepper mototr drivers
- CNC shield V3 for Arduino UNO
- Tybe B printer cable (2m)
- Jumper wires
- Limit switches
- Steel rods (as per need, I choose 16 mm diameter but it could be as low as 10 mm diameter)
- Linear ball bearings (as per choosed rod diameter)
- Axial(normal) ball bearings (as per lead screw diameter)
- Aluminium extrusions
- T nuts for the aluminium extrusions
- Lead screws with lead screw nuts (8mm diameter)
- Motor shaft to lead screw couplings
- 3D printed parts (adjusted to the changes made above
- Various lengths and sizes of screws
- 12 volt, 10 amp or 24 volt, 10 amp power supply
- Spindle motor
- Spindle motor driver
- Power supply for the spindle depending on it's rating
- ER11 collet and chuck for the spindle
- various collets for various endmill shaft diameters
- CNC endmills compatible with the ER11 collet you have
- sacrificial wooden base

## Firmware
- GRBL 1.1

## Software
- Universal Gcode Sender (latest version)
- FreeCAD 1.0 or any other CAM software
- Inkscape
